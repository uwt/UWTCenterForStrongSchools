About Us

Our team leads schools and facilities to continuously improve and sustain a system of supports in order meet the social, emotional, and learning needs of all youth, every day.

Gregory J. Benner, Ph.D., Professor & Executive Director

Greg Benner is a Professor of Education at the University of Washington – Tacoma and Executive Director of the UWT Center for Strong Schools (CSS), and specializes in preventive approaches for meeting the academic and social/emotional needs of students, particularly those with emotional and behavioral disorders (EBD). As a parent of four energetic kids, he has expertise in building the capacity of educators, mental health professionals, and parents to better understand and meet the needs of youth who are least understood and struggling most. He has consulted in hundreds of schools and facilities to build a sustainable, comprehensive multi-tiered system of support. He has a knack for collective impact—getting whole communities including families, child welfare, mental health, social and health services, and schools on the same page to meet needs of the whole child. In 2002, he was awarded the Wesley Becker Award for Outstanding Research. His book entitled Instructional Practices for Students with Behavioral Disorders: Strategies for Reading, Writing, and Math is part of the What Works for Special Needs Learners Series published by Guilford Press. He recently served as Principal Investigator on an Institute of Education Sciences-funded Goal 3 Efficacy Study. He currently serves as Associate Editor for Behavioral Disorders and Remedial and Special Education, and on the editorial review board for the Journal of Emotional and Behavioral Disorders and the Journal of Behavioral Education. He has over 200 presentations and publications that reflect his ability to disseminate research findings and best practices to the field.

Elizabeth M. Coker, Ph.D., M.S.Ed., Director of Community Research & Development

Liz Coker is the director of Community Research and Development at the UWT Center for Strong Schools. She has MA degree in Psychology, and Ph.D in anthropology, and a Master of Science in Education, as well as many years of experience developing and evaluating community-based programs for vulnerable and/or at-risk populations. She has served as the principal investigator on several government-funded oversees projects including community-based needs assessments, refugee health practices, and reproductive health initiatives. She served as the director of evaluation for a New York City-based social service organization serving the homeless, at-risk pre-school children and their families, senior citizens, and individuals with chronic mental illness. She has produced a series of reports on educational achievement and social risk factors for youth served by the Washington State Dept. of Social and Health Services using one of the first cross-agency statewide longitudinal data systems in the country. She is currently managing the “Strengthening Education in Juvenile Justice Centers” grant, focusing on effective educational service delivery in short-term juvenile detention centers in Washington State. She is committed to integrating sound research and practice to help students to reach success and thrive regardless of their current or past challenges.
